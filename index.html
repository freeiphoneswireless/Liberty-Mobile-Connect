Who is usually eligible

Lifeline supports one service per household. People may qualify through program participation or through income. Program examples include SNAP, Medicaid, SSI, Federal Public Housing Assistance, Veterans Pension or Survivors Benefit, and certain Tribal programs. Income rules vary by household size, your state page explains the exact limits.

How to apply in three simple steps

  1. Create your National Verifier account
    Go to the official portal, upload your documents, and submit. Keep your confirmation handy.
    Open the National Verifier
  2. Choose a participating provider in your state
    Use the state list on Lifeline Support Center, compare coverage and device policies, then enroll with that provider.
    Find companies near you
  3. Activate service and keep your benefit active
    Use your device regularly, respond to annual recertification requests, and update your information when your household changes.

About devices and models

Availability depends on provider inventory and state policies. Some providers offer refurbished smartphones that pass quality checks. Model names can change over time. If a site promises specific premium models for a fee, verify with official pages first.

Costs and fees

Lifeline discounts the monthly service. Device offers vary by provider. Be careful with high activation or shipping fees. If a fee looks unusual, contact the provider through an official phone number or live chat listed on their site, and avoid payments that use gift cards or peer to peer apps.

Avoid scams and protect your documents

Red flags to avoid

  • Large activation fees or payment by gift card
  • Asking for documents over direct messages on social apps
  • No privacy policy or no contact address

Check before you pay

Open the company list on Lifeline Support Center, use the official phone number or site from that list, then confirm the fee policy in writing.

If you already paid

Dispute the charge with your bank, tell the card network or payment app, and report the site to your state consumer protection office.

Frequently asked questions

ACP ended in 2024 when funding lapsed. Lifeline continues in 2025. Use the National Verifier to start. If a provider markets an ACP device deal, read their policy page carefully and look for recent updates.

Devices are usually in kind items, not income. Programs ask you to report changes like income and household size. Check your state rules or speak with your caseworker if you are not sure.

Providers supply models based on inventory. If a page guarantees a specific premium model for a high fee, verify on the provider site listed on the USAC company finder before you proceed.
